The Terracotta Army or Terracotta Warriors and Horses is a collection of 8,099 larger than life Chinese   figures of warriors and horses located near the Mausoleum of the First Qin Emperor. The Terracotta Army was    with the Emperor of Qin in 210-209 BC. Their purpose was to help rule another   with Shi Huangdi in the afterlife. The Terracotta Army was discovered in March 1974 by local farmers drilling a water well to the east of Mount Lishan. Construction of this   began in 246 BC and is believed to have taken 700,000 workers and   38 years to complete. Qin Shi Huangdi was interred inside the tomb complex upon his death in 210 BC.  The tomb of Qin Shi Huangdi is near an earthen pyramid 76 metres tall and nearly 350 square metres. The tomb presently remains   but there are plans to seal off the area around the tomb with a special tent-type structure to prevent corrosion from   to outside air. However, there is at present only one company in the world that makes these tents, and their largest model will not cover the site as needed. The   of the craftsmen working in the tomb may also be found within its confines, as it is believed they were sealed inside alive to keep them from divulging any secrets about its   or entrance. It was only fitting, therefore, to have this compound protected by the   terracotta army interred nearby.
