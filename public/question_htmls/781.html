Practice 2
Elephants, like humans, are a highly intelligent, long-lived mammalian species with strong family ties, a complex social life, and long memories. Human violence and habitat destruction have been breaking up those families. Numbers in Africa have dropped from 10 million (1900), to half million (2005). There is evidence elephants, suffer from the traumatic stress that we think of as human disorders. Early traumatic experiences can have long-lasting effects, raising the risk of post-traumatic stress disorder, depression, and violence. Today, many elephants are showing signs of abnormal startled responses, unpredictable aggression, and antisocial behaviour.

Which of the following conclusions (from the text) is best supported by the information in the above paragraph?
 
  1.  Elephants, like humans, are a highly intelligent, long-lived mammalian species with strong family ties, a complex social life, and long memories.
  2.  In 2005, after a century of ivory poaching, habitat loss, and legal culling, about a half million are left.
  3.  There is vivid evidence that other animal species, particularly elephants, suffer from the traumatic stress that we like to think of as human disorders.
  4.  Often, they have only young unexperienced mothers, or no mothers, to raise them, and there are few older males to mentor them during adolescence.
