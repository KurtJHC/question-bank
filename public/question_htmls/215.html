Everyone Loves Harold

 

1      Harold Huffington did not enjoy school. He struggled in history and geography, felt baffled in math, and made a mockery of the guitar in music class. The only subjects he liked were gym and lunch, especially every other Friday’s Pizza Day.

2      Almost every day, Harold’s mama said, “Harold Huffington, if you want to grow up to be an accountant or a mortician, you must be at school on time, listen to your teachers, do your homework, and make good grades. Then you will have a great career.”

3      And almost every day, Harold’s papa said, “That boy doesn’t need to search for a career because he is going to inherit the family business, Mr. and Mrs. Hubert Huffington’s Dry Cleaners and Wash-a-Rama.”

4      However, every time Mama and Papa discussed Harold’s future, Harold thought of something else. He did not want to be an accountant or a mortician. He didn’t want to take over the family business. No, Harold dreamed of becoming only one thing: a television star.

5      Almost every day, Harold was late to school. He missed announcements in homeroom, forgot to do his homework, and studied for tests an hour before test time. Harold didn’t care because he knew he would soon be starring in his own sitcom, Everyone Loves Harold; his own game show, Would You Like to be a Zillionaire?; or his own talk show, Harold Huffington Chats with the World’s Most Important People.

6      Then, on one Friday’s Pizza Day, Harold sat in Mr. Schwartz’s music class, thinking about lunch and whether he would choose pepperoni, sausage, or mushrooms. He pretended to enjoy Mr. Schwartz’s favourite CD of a boys’ choir singing “When Bluebirds Fly O’er the White Cliffs of Cleveland.” The music droned on while Mr. Schwartz sat in quiet contemplation of the melody as Harold and the rest of the class watched the clock. There were only five minutes to go before the lunch bell would ring, but it seemed like forever.

7      At last the lunch bell rang. Suddenly Mr. Schwartz sprang out of his seat and yelled, “Surprise test tomorrow, boys and girls! Study hard tonight!” Harold did not hear this but instead pushed past the other students, out the door, and down the hall.

What does Harold have in common with the other students in Mr. Schwartz’s class?
  1. They want music class to end.
  2. They only pretend to enjoy the music.
  3. They daydream about what kind of pizza they will eat.
  4. They do not hear Mr. Schwartz speak at the end of class.
