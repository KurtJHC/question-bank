70914

Read the text below then answer the questions.

Once again I felt myself frozen by the sense of something irreparable. And I knew that I could not bear the thought of never hearing that laughter anymore. For me, it was like a spring of fresh water in the desert.

“Little man,” I said, “I want to hear you laugh again.”

But he said to me:

“Tonight, it will be a year . . . My star, then, can be found right above the place where I came to the Earth, a year ago . . .”

“Little man,” I said, “tell me that it is only a bad dream--this affair of the snake, and the meeting-place, and the star . . .”

But he did not answer my plea. He said to me, instead:

“The thing that is important is the thing that is not seen . . .”

“Yes, I know . . .”

“It is just as it is with the flower. If you love a flower that lives on a star, it is sweet to look at the sky at night. All the stars are a-bloom with flowers . . .”

“Yes, I know . . .”

“It is just as it is with the water. Because of the pulley, and the rope, what you gave me to drink was like music. You remember--how good it was.”

“Yes, I know . . .”

"And at night you will look up at the stars. Where I live everything is so small that I cannot show you where my star is to be found. It is better, like that. My star will just be one of the stars, for you. And so you will love to watch all the stars in the heavens . . . they will all be your friends. And, besides, I am going to make you a present . . .”

He laughed again.

“Ah, little prince, dear little prince! I love to hear that laughter!” 

“That is my present. Just that. It will be as it was when we drank the water . . .” 

“What are you trying to say?”

“All men have the stars,” he answered, “but they are not the same things for different people. For some, who are travellers, the stars are guides. For others they are no more than little lights in the sky. For others, who are scholars, they are problems. For my businessman they were wealth. But all these stars are silent. You -- you alone -- will have the stars as no one else has them--” 

“What are you trying to say?”

“In one of the stars I shall be living. In one of them I shall be laughing. And so it will be as if all the stars were laughing, when you look at the sky at night . . . You--only you--will have stars that can laugh!” 

And he laughed again. 

“And when your sorrow is comforted (time soothes all sorrows) you will be content that you have known me. You will always be my friend. You will want to laugh with me. And you will sometimes open your window, so, for that pleasure . . . And your friends will be properly astonished to see you laughing as you look up at the sky! Then you will say to them, ‘Yes, the stars always make me laugh!’ And they will think you are crazy. It will be a very shabby trick that I shall have played on you . . .” 

And he laughed again.

“It will be as if, in place of the stars, I had given you a great number of little bells that knew how to laugh . . .” 

And he laughed again. Then he quickly became serious: 

“Tonight--you know . . . Do not come.”

In the text, how does the little prince describe the stars to the narrator?
 
  1. as a source of wealth and materialistic value.
  2. as personal and unique symbols of emotional connection.
  3. as scientific phenomena to be studied.
  4. as navigational tools for travellers.

THE GOD OF MUSIC

The God of Music dwells out of doors.
All seasons through his minstrelsy we meet,
Breathing by field and covert haunting-sweet
From organ-lofts in forests old he pours:
A solemn harmony: on leafy floors
To smooth autumnal pipes he moves his feet,
Or with the tingling plectrum of the sleet
In winter keen beats out his thrilling scores.
Leave me the reed unplucked beside the stream.
And he will stoop and fill it with the breeze;
Leave me the viol’s frame in secret trees,
Unwrought, and it shall wake a druid theme;
Leave me the whispering shell on Nereid shores.
The God of Music dwells out of doors.

ENGLISH EDITH M. THOMAS.
*Neveid – magical, where sea-nymphs are found

Breathing by field and covert haunting-sweet
From organ-lofts in forests old he pours:
A solemn harmony: on leafy floors
To smooth autumnal pipes he moves his feet,
Or with the tingling plectrum of the sleet
In winter keen beats out his thrilling scores.
Leave me the reed unplucked beside the stream.
And he will stoop and fill it with the breeze;
Leave me the viol’s frame in secret trees,
Unwrought, and it shall wake a druid theme;
Leave me the whispering shell on Nereid shores.
The God of Music dwells out of doors.

Recycling is an essential practice that helps protect the environment by reducing waste, conserving resources, and minimising pollution. It involves collecting used materials such as paper, plastic, glass, and metals and processing them into new products. (A) ……….. As the global demand for products continues to rise, recycling has become increasingly important in promoting sustainability and reducing the negative impact of human activity on the planet. By understanding the benefits of recycling and making an effort to recycle properly, individuals and communities can contribute to a healthier environment.

One of the key benefits of recycling is the conservation of natural resources. Every year, vast amounts of trees are cut down, metals are mined, and fossil fuels are burned to produce new materials. Recycling reduces this demand by allowing used materials to be repurposed instead of being discarded. For example, recycling paper helps prevent deforestation, preserving forests that serve as habitats for wildlife and play a crucial role in maintaining biodiversity. Similarly, recycling metals such as aluminium significantly reduces the need for mining, which can lead to habitat destruction, soil erosion, and pollution. (B).…………  

Recycling also plays a crucial role in reducing pollution. When waste is sent to landfills, harmful chemicals can seep into the soil and water, posing a threat to the environment and human health. Additionally, burning waste releases toxic gases into the atmosphere, contributing to air pollution and climate change. (C) ……….. This not only reduces pollution but also lowers the carbon footprint associated with waste disposal. By properly recycling materials, we can help keep our air, water, and land clean while reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Another major advantage of recycling is energy conservation. Producing new materials from raw resources requires significant amounts of energy, whereas recycling uses far less. For example, recycling aluminium saves up to 95% of the energy needed to produce new aluminium from raw bauxite ore. These energy savings contribute to a decrease in carbon emissions, making recycling an effective way to combat climate change. Furthermore, recycling supports the development of a circular economy, where products are designed to be reused and recycled rather than discarded. (D) ………… 

(E) ………… One of the biggest issues is contamination, which occurs when non recyclable items are mixed with recyclables. Public education is vital in addressing this issue, as people need clear guidelines on how to recycle correctly. Another challenge is the fluctuating demand for recycled materials, which can make recycling economically difficult. Governments and businesses can help by creating stable markets for recycled goods and implementing policies that encourage recycling.

For recycling to be effective, individuals, communities, and governments must all take responsibility. Individuals can contribute by sorting waste correctly and reducing unnecessary waste, such as excessive packaging. (F) ………. These include using more recyclable materials and reducing single-use plastics. Governments play a key role by providing proper recycling infrastructure, enforcing recycling policies, and supporting initiatives that encourage waste reduction. Through collective action, recycling can become a more efficient and impactful practice, helping to protect the environment and ensure a sustainable future for generations to come.
