Rookie survives early scare

November 28, 2007 - 3:35PM

 

Rookie sensation Stephanie Gilmore survived an early scare to inch her way towards the world surfing title at the Roxy Pro in Hawaii today. Gilmore could become the first female rookie in surfing history to claim the world title if she can win the Roxy Pro, but after a horror first round loss in two metre waves today the Gold Coast teenager had to fight through the repechage.

 

But the gun 19-year-old overcame her early defeat to Hawaiian wildcard Coco Ho and Australian Claire Bevilacqua to post the day's highest heat score in a dominant second round win.

 

Gilmore scored 18.10 (out of 20) heat score and the highest single wave score of 9.60 to defeat Rochelle Ballard (Hawaii) and Jacqueline Silva (Brazil) and move into the quarter-finals.

"It's all just a whirlwind in my head," Gilmore said after facing just her second repechage round this year. "There are so many things I'm thinking about and so many things that I shouldn't be thinking about. I think I was just a little bit rattled in my first heat, I couldn't find any waves. My round two heat was good. I felt like it was just another chance to get some more practice with my boards. Sunset can be pretty challenging.

 

"I didn't really think I was going to make it. I felt like I was going to eat it pretty hard," she said of her highest scoring ride. "Luckily I managed to make it down the face, pull myself together and get a few really good turns in."

 

Gilmore's first round victor Ho, however, was breathing easy after seeing her idol advance to the quarters where they'll meet again along with Aussies Jessi Miley-Dyer and Rebecca Woods.

 

"Stephanie Gilmore is my ultimate hero," Ho said.

"It was the best experience to be able to surf with her, but I was so bummed that she wasn't able to get a few good ones and put on a show for us. I'm rooting for her for the world title race for sure all the way."

The Redback Leftovers

By Debra Oswald

 

No sane person could describe the Redback Bay Under 13 Leftovers as a team really. Imagine the worst soccer team you ever saw and then triple it. Let me review the lineup of players to give you some idea.

 

Shane Borman (the fridge with a head) was a pretty good player - except for one problem. He kept growling and thumping everyone else on the field. Even players on his own team.

Someoned bump Shane accidentally and hed aim his fist. Want me to thump ya? Do ya? I will. Ill thump ya, hed roar. And you had no trouble believing him.

Robbie Oldroyd copped quite a few of Shanes threats. Something about him particularly riles Shane up. Robbie would just dance off, cackling.

Robbie went to my school, so I already knew he wasnt on the same planet as other people. For a start, he was always singing to himself. In the middle of a soccer game hed lose interest, wander off and swing back and forth on the goalposts or beat out a rhythm on the crossbar like it was a set of drums. Robbie made me laugh and he was never nasty - but I can see why he drove a lot of people crazy.

 

Mozzie was so small and scrawny, youd pick him for maybe six years old. It was hard to believe he was nearly thirteen. You had to watch where you were going so you didnt tread on tiny Mozzie.

 

That little guy was certainly fast, with decent ball skills. When I first saw him streak ahead of the pack, dribbling the ball, I thought - yes, one good player. But two seconds later, Mozzie got tackled and pulverised by one of the bigger players. He lay on the ground, too scared to get up, so small you could hardly see him above the stalks of grass.

 

There was a beefy girl called Sarah. Seriously tall, huge shoulders, legs like tree trunks. She was pretty slow and lumbering on the field. Sarah ended up in the Leftovers because shed quit the girls team. I think she was sick of getting teased about being big and all that.

Anthony Zammit had been a hopeless player when he was five years old. But now he was thirteen, he was even worse.

 

Anthonys speciality was the air-kick where hed swipe at the ball but only connect with the air and end up landing on his bum. But the thing about Anthony was that he didnt care. He kept on hauling himself to his feet, still grinning. He was having a fantastic time, no matter how lousy he played. Anthony Zammit loved soccer more than any kid I ever met.

 

And Spencer - well, I hate to say it but Spencer was hopeless. Spencer kept trying these tricky manoeuvres but hed always trip on his own feet and collapse face first in the dirt. Then Shane would run right over the top of him, grinding his big boot into Spencers back.

 

We had one player, Oliver, who didnt even bother running after the ball. He just trudged grimly across the field, muttering to himself. Oliver had never played soccer in his life. He was only there because hes done a deal with his mum to give one sport a go for a season.

 

At least Oliver turned up. Holly hardly ever showed. She always came running in late to training, panting and lopsided - with one soccer boot on, the other boot flapping in her hand. One side of her hair in a plait, the rest blowing loose. Too flustered and panicky to concentrate on soccer.

 

Holly had wanted to be in the girls team but she was too disorganised to register in time. Thats how shed ended up a Leftover.

Okay, so we did have a few good players. Apart from Stefan, we had Maria. She had speed, brilliant ball skills and fierce determination. Marias old club never gave girls a decent chance to make the First Grade. She got so mad she switched clubs. Now Maria was out to prove something. Trouble was, she got stuck in the Leftovers.
