The Kangaroo
 

I am the kangaroo.                          1
Slate-grey, and red;
And when, as oft I do, 
I lift my head
Against the far-off blue
Of sky and earth,
I am a symbol too,
of this land’s birth.

I am the rock and the tree,            2
The wide plains dry,
The gorges wild and free
The blue-hot sky.
The blue-grey greenery – 
Gully and rise, 
the Aboriginal
And his far eyes.

The silence am I,                              3
The granite peak, 
The flood, the river high,
The dried-up creek,
The hot white clouds that lie
Before the sun, 
The breeze that rustles by 
Where grasses run.

I lift my head, and so                         4
Time there is writ, 
Ages of long ago
Are held in it.
I am a statue, oh, 
As old as stone:
Grey boulder stooping low, 
Standing alone.

I am the dry, hot land,                       5
The sand, the clay, 
The burning wind that fanned 
Some far-off day:
I, kangaroo, as planned,
Still, still unspent, 
The breath, the bone, the strand
Of our continent.
