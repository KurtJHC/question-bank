{^ Extract A ^}
I have always wanted to go to a Six Flags theme park. My brother thinks they are the best in the world with the scariest, biggest and most challenging rides. I’ll admit it - I’m a bit of an adrenaline junkie! I just love the feeling in the pit of my stomach at the top of a rollercoaster and the wonderful thrill as I charge down the steep incline - my hair blown back by a furious wind which howls in my ears. If I get good grades this semester, dad said he might take me there to celebrate - I really hope so!

{^ Extract B ^}
Six Flags Entertainment Corporation has 27 theme parks across America, Mexico and Canada, making it the largest theme park organisation in the world. The amusement corporation has its headquarters in Arlington, Texas. The name originated from the six different nations that have governed Texas: Spain, France, Mexico, The Republic of Texas, the United States and the Confederate States of America.

Six Flags owns theme parks, amusement parks and water parks. What’s the difference you may ask? Well, a theme park is a type of amusement park (a park designed for entertainment with rides, games and food), but it is built around a theme - like Disneyland or Legoland. All the rides and entertainment in the park are somehow related to that theme. A water park is, as its name suggests, has its rides all involve water.

{^ Extract C ^}
The last few weeks have been relentless in tragic reports from a local amusement park, reporting four hospitalisations for park guests. The park has seen four major attractions being shut down in the last three works due to safety incidents.

A young girl was taken to hospital after the lap bar holding her to the seat malfunctioned and sent her toppling out of the Ragged Racer Ride. The Mariner tipped over one boat full of riders, sending two people to hospital to be assessed for injuries. Another park enthusiast was injured after a rollercoaster became derailed. The park owners have declined to make a statement.
 

{^ Extract D ^}
Why do people enjoy rollercoaster rides?
Some people, especially thrill-seeking extroverts, seem to enjoy rollercoasters because they get to experience the physical sensations of fear while knowing on some level that they are in fact safe (putting aside rare accidents). Psychologists refer to this enjoyment of sensations that are usually considered negative as ‘benign masochism ’and it seems to be a uniquely human phenomenon. In the case of rollercoasters, these sensations are fear- related, but other examples of benign masochism include the enjoyment of sad and scary films, disgusting jokes and painfully spicy chillies. The enjoyment of rollercoasters may be distinct from the pleasure some people get from extreme sports where the fear and risk of danger is entirely real.
