South Africa raced to become the first in line.

For his first attempt at a heart transplant, Barnard selected as the recipient Louis Washkansky, a 54-year-old grocer with severe coronary artery disease. Then on 2 December 1967, opportunity came with the tragedy of 25-year-old Denise Darvall. 3 ...... Barnard’s transplant team gained permission from Darvall’s father to use her heart. The entire procedure took nine hours and involved a team of 30 professionals. The recipient patient only lived 18 days with his new heart, succumbing to pneumonia while on a hefty immunosuppressive regimen. His short survival prompted doubts about the true value of the procedure. Critics felt Barnard did not deserve the acclaim when Dr Shumway (American) had done the bulk of the background research.

Barnard became an overnight international celebrity. A New York Times article captured public sentiment with these words: “The world was startled to learn that the human heart, traditional seat of the emotions, home of the soul, symbol of love, could be transplanted from one person to another as though it were nothing more than a complicated piece of plumbing.” Barnard’s picture graced the covers of magazines such as Time, and he accepted invitations worldwide to meet dignitaries and heads of states.
