The underground opal mining town has barely changed since it was first settled around 100 years ago.

There are still no police, food deliveries come only once a week and residents still need back up generators as the electricity on the new grid conks out about once a week.

It is this simpler, remote and unique lifestyle that has made some interesting individuals leave big cities around the world and settle in this town, which boasts of being 300km from the nearest set of traffic lights.

Yet it’s not only the isolation hat relaxes locals and visitors alike. Guests at the White Cliffs Underground Motel often tell tales of deep sleeps in the peace of their underground room or the soothing effect of the even temperature that remains at 22 degrees Celsius all year round.

  4.  built from stone
