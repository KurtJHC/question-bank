Read the four extracts below on the theme of fruit picking. 

For questions 1 – 10, choose the option (A, B, C or D) which you think best answers the question.

Which extract…

is an informative text intended for tourists wanting to visit farms? 
 

  1. Extract A

    Sydney fruit picking is such a fun activity to do with kids and there’s so much on offer. From Bilpin apple picking to strawberry picking, Sydney has a wealth of delicious fresh produce for you to enjoy. Fruit picking near Sydney typically means cherries and berries of all types in summer, apples and chestnuts in autumn, mandarins and oranges in winter, and peaches and nectarines in spring. Some fruits like apples have seasons that last for months, but cherry picking near Sydney means being ready to go in November or December, while Sydney strawberry picking usually only happens over the summer months. Fruit picking season in Sydney is entirely weather dependent, so make sure you give the orchard a call before setting off.

  2. Extract B

    One of my fondest memories as a kid was picking strawberries and raspberries with my mum, sister and grandma. And, even before my daughter was born almost four years ago, I daydreamed about the time when she'd be old enough to pick fruit on a farm with me. This weekend was the first opportunity to enjoy the strawberry harvest, which will continue for a few weeks. And it was just about as fun as I remembered. What I didn't remember is how early you have to get to the farm, since picking ends when the day's supply is gone. Barthel's opens at 7 a.m., and we arrived at 10:15 a.m. The picking hotline couldn't tell me if they'd still be open this late, so we made it to Mequon as quickly as possible. Fortunately, there were plenty of berries left to be picked – especially considering my daughter's attention span for plucking strawberries lasted about 20 minutes. But she was certainly old enough to grasp the concept: Look for the biggest, reddest strawberries and throw them in the basket. Eat a couple along the way to make sure they're sweet (and they are). Stay in your lane. Be gentle with the fruit.

  3. Extract C

    Our childhood memories of summer always included the much-anticipated strawberry picking outing; bent over the long rows of plants in the warm afternoon sunshine, searching for the shock of red between the green leaves, then plucking the warm fruit from its stem. More often than not, we gobbled the sweet berries there and then, sticky juice running down our fingers and our chins, greedily bending down in search of more. The farmers always joked they should weigh us on the way in and out, but their good nature of our enjoyment of this most precious bounty of summer seemed to always get us off the hook. We left laden with punnets of sweet smelling fruit, eager to enjoy with lashings of cream after dinnertime. 

  4. Extract D

    The different types of fruit picking: You can do selective picking. For example, colour picking, where you only pick fruit with a certain colour (riper). Or where you only pick fruits of a certain size (size picking). The farmer will certainly show you samples of what needs to be picked up. This, therefore, implies smaller quantities picked than during a massive pickup, and will, therefore, have an influence on your salary if you are paid by the yield. Mass harvesting consists of picking up almost all the fruit produced by the tree. Only damaged fruits are removed. Easier than selective collection, this picking generally takes place at the end of the harvest period.
