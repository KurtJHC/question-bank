Please help to Protect Koala Habitat

To all Students in our School
1.The recent announcement that koalas will be finally listed as endangered animals is a huge wake up call for us all to protect koala habitat in New South Wales. 

2.On her recent visit to our school, Nature Conservation Council Chief, Jacqui Mumford, said. “The devastating endangered listing of koalas comes as no surprise because the state government refuses to protect habitat. Koala numbers have been falling for years and the NSW Government must act now to protect their habitat. The reality is koalas are dwindling across New South Wales and we don’t have a proper process to protect their habitat.”  

3.“If you want to save koalas you have to protect their trees. It is not complex. But koala habitat continues to be destroyed. This is because of weak government policy that prioritises land clearance for grazing, agriculture, urbanisation, timber harvesting and mining.”  

4.“The recently released NSW Koala Strategy is inadequate for protecting the species and bringing this iconic species back to a healthy population. Any party that wants to lead NSW into the future needs to have this as a commitment.” 

5.We, as your SRC are supporting the following petition to the NSW Government.

6.“We are calling on the NSW Government to immediately: ban the destruction of koala habitat, on both public and private land; end native forest logging; and expand the National Parks areas to protect high quality koala habitat, including the proposed Great Koala National Park.”   

7.We need your help! Please sign our petition. We have placed the petition forms outside the biology department. Please make sure you do this before the end of this week. We can make a difference!

Your SRC Committee members

  1. the beginning of a new era 
  2. the end of a difficult period in time
  3. an urgent need to find face the problem 
  4. putting your own personal dreams aside
