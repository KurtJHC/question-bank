An airborne disease, it is primarily spread through the coughing and sneezing of infected patients or through contact with the rash secretions. Before the rash appears, a person with chicken pox is infectious from one to five days and after the appearance of the rash the contagious period continues for four to five days. Generally, it takes 10 to 21 days for a person to develop chicken pox after contact with an infected person. Symptoms of chicken pox include nausea, fever, sore throat, pain in both ears, rash, headache, and fever.

Chicken pox has a high prevalence in the 4-10 years old age group and is one of the classic diseases of childhood. Typically, the disease is more severe in adults and it is generally more severe in adult males. Though rarely fatal, pregnant women are at a high risk of serious complication. Chicken pox has also been observed in primates such as chimpanzees and gorillas.
