The Olympic Games? Well, it was a nice idea, but it’s run its course and it’s time for it to go. Admittedly, that’s a pretty bold statement, but there are a number of reasons why the Games need to come to an end. When Pierre de Coubertin instigated the modern
Olympics in 1896, he did so hoping that by bringing amateur sportsmen (not women) together from around the world, it would help promote peace and unity. 12 .......... However, at the time, the majority of sports were amateur and not organised well enough on a global stage for their own world championships.

But, it’s no longer 1896. Organised sports have matured. Sports that are popular have flourished, ones that aren’t have been slotted into a category called ‘minor sports’. 13 .......... They have their own world cups, world championships, grand slams or world series that suit their needs and bring in money and the Olympics are a distraction that risks diluting the importance of their own events.

For smaller sports, the Olympics artificially and temporarily over-inflate their importance. Apart from the people involved, their families and a small group of others, hardly anyone cares about shooting, fencing, modern pentathlon, diving and archery. 14 .......... Many of the venues for the 2016 Rio Games now stand obsolete and are heading to the same decrepit state that buildings for the Athens and Beijing Games are in.

In 2024 break dancing will be included and there have been calls for the Fortnite video game to be added.
