The Space Race competition gained Western public attention with the ‘Sputnik crisis’, when the USSR achieved the first successful satellite launch into space, Sputnik 1, on October 4, 1957. The Space Race gained momentum when the USSR sent the first human, Yuri Gagarin, into space with the orbital flight of Vostok 1 on April 12, 1961. These were followed by a string of other firsts achieved by the Soviets over the next few years. Gagarin’s flight led US President John F. Kennedy to raise the stakes on May 25, 1961, by asking the US Congress to commit to the goal of “landing a man on the Moon and returning him safely to the Earth” before the end of the decade. Both countries began developing super heavy-lift launch vehicles, with the US successfully deploying the Saturn V, which was large enough to send a three-person orbiter and two-person lander to the Moon. Kennedy’s Moon landing goal was achieved in July 1969, with the flight of Apollo 11. The successful conclusion of Apollo 11 is regarded by most as ending the Space Race with an American victory.
