Antarctica

 

Antarctica is the fifth largest continent. Its landmass is almost wholly covered by a vast ice sheet. Often described as a continent of superlatives, Antarctica is the world’s southernmost continent. 1 ………… . Antarctica is about 14.2 million square km in size, and thick ice covers about 98 percent of the land. The continent is divided into East Antarctica (which is largely composed of a high icecovered plateau) and West Antarctica (which is largely an ice sheet covering an archipelago of mountainous islands).

 

Lying almost concentrically around the South Pole, Antarctica’s name means “opposite to the Arctic”. It would be essentially circular except for the outflaring Antarctic Peninsula, which reaches toward the southern tip of South America (some 970 km away), and for two principal embayments, the Ross Sea and the Weddell Sea. These deep embayments of the Southern Ocean make the continent somewhat pear-shaped. 2 ………… . East Antarctica lies mostly in the east longitudes and is larger than West Antarctica, which lies wholly in the west longitudes. East and West Antarctica are separated by the approximately 3,400 km long Transantarctic Mountains.

 

The continental ice sheet contains approximately 29 million cubic km of ice. It represents about 90 percent of the world’s ice and 80 percent of its fresh water. Its average thickness is about 1,800 metres. Ice shelves, or ice sheets floating on the sea, cover many parts of the Ross and Weddell seas. These shelves are called the Ross Ice Shelf and the Filchner-Ronne Ice Shelf. 3 ………… . Around the Antarctic coast, shelves, glaciers, and ice sheets continually “calve”, or discharge, icebergs into the seas.

 

4 ………… . The terrestrial ecosystem contains more than a thousand known species of organisms. Most of these are microorganisms. Maritime Antarctica—the islands and coasts—supports more life than inland Antarctica. The surrounding ocean is as rich in life as the inland land is barren.

 

About 45 species of birds live south of the Antarctic Convergence. However, only three - the Emperor penguin, Antarctic petrel, and South Polar skua - breed exclusively on the continent or on nearby islands. Penguins symbolise this polar region. Of the 18 living species, only the Adélie and emperor live along the Antarctic coastline. The habitats of five other polar species—king, chinstrap, gentoo, rockhopper, and macaroni—extend only as far south as the northern Antarctic Peninsula and subantarctic islands. Some authorities believe that penguins may have a shared ancestry with other birds of Antarctica, capable of flight, from the order Procellariiformes. Birds of that order, mainly species of petrels and albatrosses, make up more than half of the Antarctic and subantarctic breeding species. 5 ………… .

 

Antarctic native mammals are all marine and include seals (pinnipeds), porpoises, dolphins, and whales (cetaceans). Only one fur seal breeds south of the Antarctic Convergence. Four species of true seals - the Weddell seal, the crabeater seal, the carnivorous leopard seal, and the rarely seen Ross seal - breed almost exclusively in the Antarctic zone. 6 ………… . The sea lion, is plentiful in the Falkland Islands but probably never ventures into the cold Antarctic waters. The fur seal and the elephant seal are now regenerating after near extinction. Weddell seals are thought to number about 1,000,000, the crabeater about 8,000,000 and the Ross seals between 50,000 and 220,000.

 

1 …………
 
  1.  Together with other shelves around the continental margins, they fringe about 45 percent of Antarctica. 
  2.  And another, the southern elephant seal, breeds near the Convergence at South Georgia, Kerguelen, and Macquarie islands.
  3.  It is divided it into two unequal-sized parts. 
  4.  Other birds of the region include species of cormorants, pintails, gulls, terns, sheathbills, and pipits. 
  5.  Antarctica is not a country: it has no government and no indigenous population. 
  6.  But it is also the world’s highest, driest, windiest, coldest and iciest continent. 
  7.  The continent is a cold dry desert where access to water determines the abundance of life.
