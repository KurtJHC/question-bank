Practice 3
The fossilised bones of a dinosaur were discovered over a nest of fossilised eggs in the 1920s. The dinosaur was holding an egg in its mouth when it was preserved as a fossil. This must mean that the dinosaur was trying to eat those eggs.

What is the flaw in reasoning?
 
  1.  We do not know what type of dinosaur it was. 
  2.  We do not know if the whole species ate eggs or if it was only that dinosaur. It is possible that this feeding behaviour is rare, so we cannot draw conclusions from one example.
  3.  We assume that the eggs are from a different species. It is possible that the eggs are from the same species of dinosaur.
  4.  We assume the dinosaur would not hold the egg if it was not trying to eat it. It is possible it was trying to move the egg.
