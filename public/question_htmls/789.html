Underground Town 

 

    How would you like to live underground? Many families in the town of Coober Pedy, Australia, do just that. Their houses, called dugouts, are carved out of the earth. They are similar to regular houses. They have kitchens, bedrooms, and living rooms, but they have few windows. Most of the light in the houses is artificial. It comes from lamps and overhead lights instead of direct sunlight. The roofs of the homes are made of layers and layers of dirt. 

    People in the town build dugouts because of where they live. Coober Pedy is in a desert in southern Australia. Temperatures can climb as high as 49 degrees Celsius in the summer. They can plunge as low as 0 degree Celsius in the winter. Dust storms and swarms of flies can make life miserable. Underground, though, it is always a comfortable 24 degrees Celsius. People don’t even need fans.

    Why would people want to live in such a place? Coober Pedy is an opal-mining town. Opals are colorful stones used for jewelry. The mines in Coober Pedy produce most of the world’s opals.

    Early settlers in Coober Pedy realized that they could avoid the harsh temperatures by building their homes underground. Today almost half of the 3,500 people in the town live in dugouts. Restaurants, schools, and other buildings are also underground. People in Coober Pedy enjoy their lives “down under.”

(3) It all started during a basketball game in my driveway.
(4) Carla passed the ball to me. (5) It went through my hands and right into Mr. Reeds prized rosebush. (6) I was so scared. (7) Mr. Reed rarely came out of his house.
(8) He hardly ever spoke. (9) Everyone said that he hated children.
(10) I tiptoed into Mr. Reed’s yard. (11) My hands were sweaty, and there was a big lump in my throat. (12) Suddenly I saw a large shadow creeping toward me.
(13) It was Mr. Reed. (14) “Is this your ball” he grunted. (15) As I nodded my head, I promised that it would never happen again. (16) To my surprise Mr. Reed said, “Why don’t you kids play ball over here? (17) No one has used this hoop in years. (18) It’s in better shape than yours.”
(19) My friends and me couldn’t believe it. (20) Mr. Reed was inviting us to play in his yard! (21) We’ve been playing there ever since. (22) We all learned a lesson that day. (23) It was an important lesson. (24) Don’t waste time listening to neighborhood rumors. (25) They aren’t always true. 

What change, if any, should be made in sentence 19?
 
  1.  Insert a comma after friends 
  2.  Change me to I 
  3.  Insert hardly after couldn’t 
  4.  Make no change
