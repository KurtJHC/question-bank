Read the four extracts below on the theme of being alone in winter.

Extract A

The streets are silent. From my window, I can see my whole block, still and foreign in the dark. I blink and I'm standing across the street, suddenly chilled in the morning air, wishing for a jacket. I start walking, my bare feet crunching on the thin layer of snow. Ahead I see a leather jacket, laying across a park bench along with a pair of shoes, untouched by the snow laying on the frozen ground. I smile. How lucky, I will just return them in the morning. I put on the clothes and continue down my road. Then I'm across town. I stop to look around me. Trees in the distance dance to the soft music of the wind. Buildings loom on each side of the street, giant sentinels regarding me with caution. Darting between the sentinels are their companions, the lurking, chattering, mischievous shadows. Obscuring countless possibilities. I continue on my way, drifting into the night. Then I slip back into the world of reality. I stand, stretching as I slowly walk towards my window. I shield my eyes from the sun, glancing down to the park bench where a young couple sit, enjoying the sun rise, ignoring the leather jacket and shoes residing beside them.

Extract B

A lone woman in a long crimson coat wandered down the uncrowded walkways of Walnut. Harsh winds carried the chill of the river through the city’s street and beat against her back. As she passed the steps to an elevated square, a heavy bell sounded a single note. She looked to her right, to the source of the tolling. An aging, familiar structure all in red brick, displaying a large cross, stood over her. A building she'd be more familiar with if her fortune had been better, she mused.

A flat open square stretched on to her left. Snow blanketed its patches of grass and its cobbled pathways alike. A man in the shreds of a bright yellow sleeping bag laid along the stone wall perimeter of the park. A crumpled coffee cup fell to the breeze, spilling the few coins it had gathered on his behalf.

The woman approached the plastic haven of a bus shelter. Its only other occupant turned to her as she entered. The other woman was shorter and frailer, but she bore her teeth in a discomforting grimace. She wore a floral print coat that reached down to her knees and a muddy pair of pink earmuffs, which she pulled at as she shook her head. Her breaths were long wheezing streams of frigid air in the early morning.

Extract C

I'm looking at a fierce full moon, and notice that the winter really has begun. Streets and windows covered in white. The homeless get left to die in the winters here, and they will, in a night like this. They say crime here surges in the winter. But I say, it's easier to see blood in the snow. I see a drunken man singing broken Christmas carols, limping. It's not just him. Times like this... So many thieves and murderers. They've got a free feeling to them at times like this. Looking at him, from all the way up here, I think of how small he is. He couldn't possibly be aware of his surroundings right now, but he doesn't know that the footprints he makes in the snow go away quickly, and he will too, eventually. We all will. And most of us will never make a difference. Making a difference... Is that what I'm doing? Beating freaks? Putting criminals in prison and spreading the blood of murders on the snow? I'll never know. So many men broken by the world.... So many men broken here.... What he said is right....it doesn't end. Just a circle of brutes and freaks. What am I though? I am in the circle.... I've got myself just as stuck in there as they are.... Is there anything different about me? Making a difference? I need to stop, I'm trailing off. 

Extract D

The night air formed ice to my face as puffs of smoke bellowed out from my mouth. The snow fell around me making a hushing sound, a quiet breeze. The night sky glowed in the light of the cars and buildings that littered the city that was accompanied by the constant hum of the traffic buzzing from all directions. I walked down the street towards the city’s park with the sounds of crunching under my feet. Making my way over to the park's bench, the movement of something on it caught my attention. I walked a bit closer, examining the dark brown fabric as it moved up and down slowly.

“Hello?” I whispered, as to not startle him.

He groaned while he pushed himself up to face me, leaning on his right shoulder. The face of the man was that of an old man grafted onto the body of someone younger. He looked worn before his days.

I sat beside him. We watched the snow fall collecting itself and growing more than an inch higher than it had been when I arrived. Our breaths slowly fell into rhythm with the sounds that echoed throughout the city, a paced sound that grew and shrank with the passing of each car. “Come back to my place.” I said breaking the silence between us, his ears had started to redden and his body looked stiff. He only stared back. “It’s warm, and I have a spare bedroom you could use. If you don’t like it, you don’t have to stay.” There was something in his eyes. Something of a look of wonder, or hope, or sadness. He nodded and stood.

For questions 1 – 10, choose the option (A, B, C or D) which you think best answers the question.

Extract A

The streets are silent. From my window, I can see my whole block, still and foreign in the dark. I blink and I'm standing across the street, suddenly chilled in the morning air, wishing for a jacket. I start walking, my bare feet crunching on the thin layer of snow. Ahead I see a leather jacket, laying across a park bench along with a pair of shoes, untouched by the snow laying on the frozen ground. I smile. How lucky, I will just return them in the morning. I put on the clothes and continue down my road. Then I'm across town. I stop to look around me. Trees in the distance dance to the soft music of the wind. Buildings loom on each side of the street, giant sentinels regarding me with caution. Darting between the sentinels are their companions, the lurking, chattering, mischievous shadows. Obscuring countless possibilities. I continue on my way, drifting into the night. Then I slip back into the world of reality. I stand, stretching as I slowly walk towards my window. I shield my eyes from the sun, glancing down to the park bench where a young couple sit, enjoying the sun rise, ignoring the leather jacket and shoes residing beside them.

Extract B

A lone woman in a long crimson coat wandered down the uncrowded walkways of Walnut. Harsh winds carried the chill of the river through the city’s street and beat against her back. As she passed the steps to an elevated square, a heavy bell sounded a single note. She looked to her right, to the source of the tolling. An aging, familiar structure all in red brick, displaying a large cross, stood over her. A building she'd be more familiar with if her fortune had been better, she mused.

A flat open square stretched on to her left. Snow blanketed its patches of grass and its cobbled pathways alike. A man in the shreds of a bright yellow sleeping bag laid along the stone wall perimeter of the park. A crumpled coffee cup fell to the breeze, spilling the few coins it had gathered on his behalf.

The woman approached the plastic haven of a bus shelter. Its only other occupant turned to her as she entered. The other woman was shorter and frailer, but she bore her teeth in a discomforting grimace. She wore a floral print coat that reached down to her knees and a muddy pair of pink earmuffs, which she pulled at as she shook her head. Her breaths were long wheezing streams of frigid air in the early morning.

Extract C

I'm looking at a fierce full moon, and notice that the winter really has begun. Streets and windows covered in white. The homeless get left to die in the winters here, and they will, in a night like this. They say crime here surges in the winter. But I say, it's easier to see blood in the snow. I see a drunken man singing broken Christmas carols, limping. It's not just him. Times like this... So many thieves and murderers. They've got a free feeling to them at times like this. Looking at him, from all the way up here, I think of how small he is. He couldn't possibly be aware of his surroundings right now, but he doesn't know that the footprints he makes in the snow go away quickly, and he will too, eventually. We all will. And most of us will never make a difference. Making a difference... Is that what I'm doing? Beating freaks? Putting criminals in prison and spreading the blood of murders on the snow? I'll never know. So many men broken by the world.... So many men broken here.... What he said is right....it doesn't end. Just a circle of brutes and freaks. What am I though? I am in the circle.... I've got myself just as stuck in there as they are.... Is there anything different about me? Making a difference? I need to stop, I'm trailing off. 

Extract D

The night air formed ice to my face as puffs of smoke bellowed out from my mouth. The snow fell around me making a hushing sound, a quiet breeze. The night sky glowed in the light of the cars and buildings that littered the city that was accompanied by the constant hum of the traffic buzzing from all directions. I walked down the street towards the city’s park with the sounds of crunching under my feet. Making my way over to the park's bench, the movement of something on it caught my attention. I walked a bit closer, examining the dark brown fabric as it moved up and down slowly.

“Hello?” I whispered, as to not startle him.

He groaned while he pushed himself up to face me, leaning on his right shoulder. The face of the man was that of an old man grafted onto the body of someone younger. He looked worn before his days.

I sat beside him. We watched the snow fall collecting itself and growing more than an inch higher than it had been when I arrived. Our breaths slowly fell into rhythm with the sounds that echoed throughout the city, a paced sound that grew and shrank with the passing of each car. “Come back to my place.” I said breaking the silence between us, his ears had started to redden and his body looked stiff. He only stared back. “It’s warm, and I have a spare bedroom you could use. If you don’t like it, you don’t have to stay.” There was something in his eyes. Something of a look of wonder, or hope, or sadness. He nodded and stood.

For questions 1 – 10, choose the option (A, B, C or D) which you think best answers the question.
