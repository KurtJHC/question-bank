Long-nosed fur seals 

Recently, there was a sighting of a long-nosed fur seal in Victoria. This fur seal was tagged in Tasmania when it was a pup, 25 years ago. This particular male fur seal was born on Maatsyuker Island. 12 ………. The fur seal was tagged with ‘830’ in 1996, so that researchers could track the movements of the special animal. The wildlife officers in Victoria who discovered the fur seal saw his tag and decided to find out where he came from.

When marine biologist, Sam Thalmann from the Tasmanian environment department received news that the fur seal had been sighted in Victoria, he stated, “We were excited to respond to this call and gain important updated information from research activities conducted more than 20 years ago.” 13 ………. Seeing as though this particular fur seal is now 25 years old, it has given researchers thrilling and interesting new knowledge that wild fur seals in Australia could have a longer lifespan than originally thought. Another piece of good news is that the fur seal was in great physical condition and hopefully he will do well in the years to come.

The coasts of south-eastern Australia, southern New South Wales and also New Zealand are where long-nosed fur seals can be found. Sadly, in the 1800s, these lovely seals were hunted until they were nearly extinct. 14 ………. Seal hunters sent at least 100,000 skins from the Kangaroo Island region alone, between 1800 and 1830. It took the fur seal population a long time to recover. 

In 1987, the small population of fur seals recolonised in the waters of Tasmania and formed a breeding colony on Maatsuyker Island. When the male seal who was discovered in Victoria was tagged 25 years ago, less than 100 pups were born per year. Now, there are around 117,000 fur seals swimming around Australia and New Zealand, which is an improvement but the population is still quite low. 15 ………. People face fines of up to $100,000 for killing or harming them.

If people are lucky enough to see these beautiful fur seals in the wild, they can often be seen with their flippers in the air, floating. This regulates their body temperature and is called ‘sailing’ or ‘jug handling’. Humans should never approach fur seals because they are very big and strong wild animals. 16 ………. 

It is also important to know that humans should never feed any marine mammals. Seals tend to eat squid, cuttlefish, lobster and, of course, fish. Feeding seals can cause them to be dependent on people. 17 ………. This can cause problems for the person fishing as well as the animal as injuries can occur.

Walter Elias Disney was born on 5th December 1901, in Chicago, USA. He was a motion picture and television producer as well as a pioneer of cartoon films. On 15th December 1966, Disney sadly died from lung cancer. In the late 1920s, the cartoons featuring Mickey Mouse and his friends were created. The first successful film starring the lovable Mickey Mouse was called Steamboat Willie.
